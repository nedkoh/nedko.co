# Hilton Head Island Golf Courses Guide

Hilton Head Island is one of America's premier golf destinations, featuring **24 championship courses** designed by legendary architects including Pete Dye, Jack Nicklaus, Robert Trent Jones Sr., Arthur Hills, and George Fazio.

## Why Golf on Hilton Head Island?

Despite being just 12 miles long and 5 miles wide, Hilton Head Island packs an incredible concentration of world-class golf:

- **24 championship courses** in one destination
- **PGA TOUR venue** hosting the RBC Heritage since 1969
- **Legendary course designers** with diverse playing experiences
- **Year-round golf** with mild Lowcountry weather
- **Stunning scenery** with ocean views, marshlands, and ancient oaks

## Top Golf Courses on Hilton Head Island

### Harbour Town Golf Links

**The crown jewel of Hilton Head golf**

Harbour Town Golf Links is one of the most iconic courses in American golf, hosting the PGA TOUR's RBC Heritage tournament every spring since 1969.

**Designer:** Pete Dye with Jack Nicklaus consulting

**Key Features:**
- Iconic red and white striped lighthouse at the 18th hole
- Tight, tree-lined fairways requiring precision
- Small, well-protected greens
- Lowcountry marsh and Calibogue Sound views

**Course Stats:**
- Par: 71
- Yardage: 6,973 (Championship)
- Rating/Slope: 74.0/136

**Best for:** Bucket-list golfers, PGA TOUR fans, experienced players

**Access:** Guests of Sea Pines Resort or tee time packages

---

### Atlantic Dunes at Sea Pines

**Historic course with modern design**

Atlantic Dunes is a complete reconstruction of the Ocean Course, Hilton Head's first golf course. Designer Davis Love III created a links-style experience.

**Designer:** Davis Love III (2014 reconstruction)

**Key Features:**
- Links-style design with ocean influences
- Wide fairways with strategic bunkering
- Natural dune landscapes
- Views of the Atlantic Ocean

**Course Stats:**
- Par: 72
- Yardage: 6,900

**Best for:** Players seeking a links experience, resort guests

---

### Arthur Hills at Palmetto Dunes

**South Carolina's best resort course**

Consistently ranked among South Carolina's top public courses, Arthur Hills offers a challenging yet playable layout through maritime forests and lagoons.

**Designer:** Arthur Hills

**Key Features:**
- Ranked in "Top 30 Best Courses You Can Play in SC"
- Dramatic elevation changes
- Scenic lagoon crossings
- Excellent course conditioning

**Course Stats:**
- Par: 72
- Yardage: 6,651
- Rating/Slope: 72.2/132

**Best for:** Resort guests, mid-to-low handicappers

---

### Robert Trent Jones at Palmetto Dunes

**Classic design with Lowcountry beauty**

The RTJ Course showcases Robert Trent Jones Sr.'s signature style with dramatic bunkering and challenging water hazards.

**Designer:** Robert Trent Jones Sr.

**Key Features:**
- Signature RTJ bunkering style
- 11 holes with water hazards
- Ocean-side holes with stunning views
- Wide fairways with challenging approaches

**Course Stats:**
- Par: 72
- Yardage: 7,005
- Rating/Slope: 73.4/134

**Best for:** Traditional golf enthusiasts, resort guests

---

### George Fazio at Palmetto Dunes

**Strategic thinking golfer's course**

The Fazio Course rewards course management and strategic play over raw power.

**Designer:** George Fazio

**Key Features:**
- Premium on accuracy and course management
- Well-placed hazards
- Smaller, undulating greens
- Quieter, less crowded rounds

**Course Stats:**
- Par: 70
- Yardage: 6,873

**Best for:** Strategic players, those seeking a less crowded experience

---

### Oyster Reef Golf Club

**Stunning marsh views**

Oyster Reef offers a semi-private experience with exceptional views of Port Royal Sound and the surrounding marshlands.

**Designer:** Rees Jones

**Key Features:**
- Spectacular marsh and sound views
- Links-style holes along the water
- Well-conditioned fairways
- Welcoming to all skill levels

**Course Stats:**
- Par: 72
- Yardage: 7,027

**Best for:** Photographers, nature lovers, semi-private experience seekers

---

### Palmetto Hall Plantation

**36 holes of championship golf**

Palmetto Hall features two distinct 18-hole courses designed by Arthur Hills and Robert Cupp.

**Designers:** Arthur Hills, Robert Cupp

**Key Features:**
- Arthur Hills Course: Natural marshland setting
- Robert Cupp Course: Augusta-style design with flowering landscapes
- Semi-private access
- Excellent practice facilities

**Best for:** Golfers wanting 36-hole days, variety seekers

---

### Port Royal Golf Club

**Three courses with ocean views**

Port Royal offers three distinct courses: Barony, Planters Row, and Robber's Row.

**Key Features:**
- Ocean-view holes on Barony Course
- Three different playing experiences
- Historic plantation setting
- Westin Hilton Head guests receive preferred access

**Best for:** Westin guests, players seeking variety

---

### Shipyard Golf Club

**27 holes through maritime forests**

Shipyard's 27 holes are divided into three 9-hole courses: Clipper, Galleon, and Brigantine.

**Designers:** George Cobb, Willard Byrd

**Key Features:**
- Flexible 9-hole combinations
- Beautiful maritime forest setting
- Convenient mid-island location
- Value-oriented green fees

**Best for:** Flexible schedules, budget-conscious golfers

## Golf Packages and Tee Times

### Popular Golf Package Options

Most Hilton Head resorts offer golf packages that include:
- Accommodations
- Daily rounds at resort courses
- Preferred tee times
- Practice facility access
- Cart and range balls included

### Booking Tee Times

- **Resort guests:** Book through your resort or property manager
- **Public courses:** Book online or call course directly
- **Peak season:** Book 30-60 days in advance
- **Shoulder season:** 7-14 days typically sufficient

## Golf Tips for Hilton Head

1. **Play early or late** - Beat the heat and often find better rates
2. **Bring bug spray** - Lowcountry mosquitoes are active near marshes
3. **Expect wind** - Ocean breezes affect ball flight, especially at Sea Pines
4. **Dress code** - Most courses require collared shirts
5. **GPS devices** - Helpful for navigating tree-lined courses
